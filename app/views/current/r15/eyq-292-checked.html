Result for this qualification

Not full and relevant at either level 3 or level 6

Given the information you have provided, the qualification cannot be considered full and relevant at either level 3 or level 6.

Staff:child ratios for this qualification

As the qualification is not full and relevant, the qualification holder is not able to work as a qualified member of staff within the staff:child ratios in a registered early years setting in England. They can, however, work as an unqualified member of staff.

Alternatively, they may be eligible to count at level 3 through the experience-based route, if they meet the relevant criteria.

Level 2

Further action required

If a person holds a level 3, 4, 5, 6 or 7 qualification that is not on the early years qualifications list (EYQL), they may be able to count in the staff:child ratios at level 2 if:

  • the qualification was started between 1 September 2014 and 31 August 2019
  • the qualification is early years (0 to 5 years) related
  • the qualification is appropriate to early years practice

The Department for Education (DfE) can help you confirm whether a qualification started between 1 September 2014 and 31 August 2019 meets these requirements.

To get help with confirming these qualifications, you can use the DfE contact form (opens in new tab). Choose the option to ask a question and include 'For the attention of the Early Years Qualifications Unit' in your message. You will usually get a reply within 15 working days.

Level 3

Possible route available

The qualification holder may be eligible for the Department for Education's experience-based route (opens in new tab). This is a new process which will allow suitable early years practitioners with an approved Level 2 qualification the opportunity to gain status to work within the Level 3 staff:child ratios. This is an optional flexibility, available from 3 March 2025 to providers and educators who meet the eligibility criteria.

Level 6

Possible route available

The qualification is not approved to count in the staff:child ratios at Level 6.

To be included in the staff:child ratios at level 6, staff must hold one of the following:

  • Qualified Teacher Status (QTS)
  • Early Years Teacher Status (EYTS)
  • Early Years Professional Status (EYPS)

The qualification holder may be eligible for early years initial teacher training (EYITT) (opens in new tab). This route allows suitable early years practitioners with a Level 6 degree qualification to gain Early Years Teacher Status (EYTS) and be included in the Level 6 staff:child ratios.



Your responsibilities as a provider

As set out in the early years foundation stage (EYFS) statutory framework (opens in new tab), providers must:

  • ensure that staffing arrangements meet the needs of all children and ensure their safety
  • ensure that people looking after children are suitable
  • meet all other requirements as set out in the EYFS and the early years qualification requirements and standards document (opens in new tab)
